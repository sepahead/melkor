Product review · 2.0.0-rc.1 · 10 July 2026

Melkor now closes the first-use loop.

The product has moved from a capable engineering toolkit with a demo viewer to a coherent local workflow: create or convert a splat, validate it deterministically, and open it privately in the viewer. The next product gains should reduce repeated-work friction rather than add more reconstruction backends.

Verdict

8.1 / 10 — strong technical RC for developers and technical artists. The core promise is credible and the primary workflow is now usable without editing source code or copying assets into a public directory. Trust and reliability are unusually strong for this stage. Onboarding, batch work, share/export actions, and persistent project history remain the clearest product gaps.

What is working

Clearer promise

  • The native CLI performs honest deterministic conversion instead of exposing incomplete fit/feedforward facades.
  • Dedicated OpenSplat and pinned DA3 paths carry the learned reconstruction work.
  • melkor inspect gives CI and users an immediate preflight surface.

Local-first trust

  • Picker and drag/drop support PLY, SPZ, SPLAT, KSPLAT, SOG, and ZIP.
  • Bytes stay in the browser/webview and work offline after startup.
  • Damaged candidates preserve the current static or 4D scene and retain a usable recovery action.

Technical depth

  • Metal, CUDA, and CPU share reference semantics.
  • Scene completion, compression, active-scene glTF traversal, and bounded 4D playback cover real downstream work.
  • Malformed inputs fail with deterministic, machine-readable diagnostics.

Credible release posture

  • The RC version is synchronized across native, npm, Tauri, Cargo, lockfiles, changelog, and notices.
  • Source archives, SBOM, checksums, and provenance are reproducible and self-verifying.
  • Unsigned RC evidence is clearly distinguished from signed production distribution.

Release recommendation

Cut the first source RC after committing the reviewed tree and obtaining green tag CI. Present it as a developer/technical-artist release candidate, not a signed production desktop distribution or a research-quality benchmark claim. The next product cycle should favor batch work, camera sharing, and session continuity over adding another backend.

Audience assumed: 3DGS developers and technical artists. Review stage: source release candidate. Evidence: repository behavior, live viewer inspection, automated matrix, and current primary format/runtime documentation.
